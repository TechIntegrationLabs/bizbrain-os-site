The Full Story

I'm not a traditional software engineer. I started in mechanical engineering, drifted into electrical systems and robotics, and eventually carved out my own path in mechatronics — the intersection of hardware, software, and everything in between. I spent about six years in college across multiple schools, not chasing a degree but chasing the specific knowledge I needed to build real things. That unconventional route forced me to learn on the fly, build under pressure, and develop a mindset built on execution over theory.

That mindset showed up everywhere I worked. At The VOID, I started as a carpenter. Within days I was promoted into an engineering role. Six months later I was leading the engineering team, designing the haptic props and physical systems that brought location-based VR to life. Out of over 350 employees, I was eventually listed on the company's Wikipedia page as one of four key people, alongside the three founders.

At Dreamwalk, I was hired as a Robotics Engineer despite having never built robots before. What I had was a reputation for figuring things out. Alongside my teammates Guy and Calvin Francis, we designed and built the robotics, interactive control systems, lighting, and effects that powered the entire experience. I created "BOB the Talking Fish" — a real-time conversational animatronic powered by GPT-4 and ElevenLabs — and designed custom ESPHome relay PCBs that now drive lighting, fog, pneumatics, and show-control hardware across the venue.

"If I can imagine it, I can now code it. ChatGPT blew the software ceiling off for me — and I've been building nonstop ever since."

When GPT-3.5 dropped, I became obsessed. By mid-2024, I went all-in on AI. I founded Tech Integration Labs and started helping small and mid-sized businesses bolt AI onto their existing operations — often delivering working prototypes in under 24 hours. I built content engines, workflow orchestrators, SaaS platforms, and AI-powered tools for clients across industries from dental practices to construction companies to cultural centers.

But the real breakthrough was the system I built for myself. Managing a growing roster of clients, projects, and repos required a context layer that no existing tool provided. I needed AI that knew my business — every client, every project, every workflow, every preference — and picked up exactly where I left off every single session. So I built it. And after years of daily iteration, that system became BizBrain OS.

Why I Built BizBrain OS

Every AI tool has the same blind spot — it doesn't know your business. BizBrain OS fixes that. Not with another dashboard or chatbot, but with a structured context layer that teaches AI everything about your clients, projects, workflows, and preferences. It compounds daily. It runs locally. And it makes every AI interaction dramatically more useful because the AI finally has the context it needs.

I built BizBrain OS because I needed it. Managing a dozen clients, hundreds of repos, and thousands of files across multiple projects — I couldn't afford to re-explain my business to AI every session. The system I built for myself worked so well that I open-sourced it. It's not theoretical. It's the exact system I use every single day to run Tech Integration Labs.

If you've ever wished AI just knew your business already — that's what this is.
